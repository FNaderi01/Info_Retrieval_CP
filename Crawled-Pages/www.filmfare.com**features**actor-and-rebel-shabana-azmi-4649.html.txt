			Actor and rebel: Shabana Azmi
	by  Filmfare | November 13, 2013, 10:35 IST
						Shabana Azmi was born to talented parents who were communist by choice. Her father noted poet Kaifi Azmi, and mother actress Shauqat, both were pillars of left-wing intellectualism. It wasn’t unusual for young Shabana to get up in the morning and find members of communist intelligentsia sleeping around after a late-night bout of rousing literary discourse. Such things influenced her choices as an actor and she deliberately stayed away from glamorous roles, opting for real-life portrayals in arthouse and middle-of-the-road cinema instead. She carried forward the legacy of her parents and used her influence as an actor to champion various causes. She’s one actor who has successfully walked the line between art and mart and continues to be a firebrand even now.															
Rivals Smita Patil and Shabana worked together in Nishant, Mandi and Arth. But Shabana stole the thunder by winning the Filmfare Award for her role in Arth.Choices, choicesShabana Azmi once confessed that it was Jaya Bhaduri who inspired her to become an actor. She watched Jaya in a diploma film Suman and was blown away by her natural performance. She decided to join FTII and in 1972 topped her class.															
With mentor Shyam Benegal on the set of Junoon. It was he who gave her, her first break with Ankur. 
She was offered films by Shyam Benegal, KA Abbas and Kanti Lal Rathod. Benegal was one of her professors at FTII, whose Ankur (1974), her first release, proved to be the defining film of her career. She won the National Award for that.															
Shabana attended the Moscow Film Festival with Shashi Kapoor in 1977.
It was at FTII that she met Benjamin Gilani, her first beau. The duo got engaged but allegedly Gilani couldn’t handle her rising fame and their engagement was called off.															
A young Shabana spearheading the slum dwellers’ movement in Mumbai.
Art and the womanShe hit her peak during the ’80s,  wowing the audience with memorable performances in Sparsh (1980), Arth  (1982), Masoom (1983) and Mandi (1983), playing difficult characters  with consummate ease.
Shabana and Amitabh Bachchan holding the Filmfare trophies in 1981.It was also the period where her rivalry with another intense actress, Smita Patil, surfaced. The duo had a catfight in the press, though Shabana later regretted making disparaging remarks about Smita.															
(L) Shekhar Kapur and Shabana were in a relationship for seven years and apparently lived in for five years.(R)  She married Javed Akhtar on December 9, 1984. Javed was previously  married to Honey Irani. Shabana shares a cordial relationship with his  children Zoya and Farhan Akhtar.
On the personal front, she was involved with Shekhar Kapoor for a while but that relationship didn’t work out as well. She finally met her emotional anchor in poet-lyricist Javed Akhtar and the duo tied the knot in 1984.															
Being mobbed by the press at an event.Controversies and causesShe courted controversy by playing a lesbian in Fire (1996) and tonsuring her head to get the look right in Water (the role later went to Seema Biswas). She played a female don in Godmother (1999). She’s also been internationally recognised for her roles in Madame Sousatzka (1988), City Of Joy (1992) and the more recent The Reluctant Fundamentalist (2013).															
On a hunger strike. Take a closer look on the white board. It signifies the weight of the participants before and after the strike. Shabana weighed 59 kgs after it.Shabana didn’t let her actor persona kill the activist in her and used her clout to help slum dwellers fight for their rights. She also helms Mijwan Welfare Society (MWS), an NGO founded by Kaifi Azmi in 1993 that works for the empowerment of villagers in Mijwan in Azamgarh, UP his birthplace.															
In the Shabana starrer Namkeen, Rekha was also supposed to play a role. However, after a tiff with director Gulzar she was replaced by Sharmila Tagore. Here’s a picture of them filming a few scenes. Shabana has also become a prominent figure for the liberal Muslims. She continues to excel as an actor and an activist, inspiring others with her life choices.
