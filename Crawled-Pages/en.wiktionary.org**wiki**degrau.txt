Portuguese[edit]
Etymology[edit]
From Old Portuguese degraao, from Vulgar Latin *dēgradus, from Latin gradus, from Proto-Indo-European *gʰredʰ- (“to walk, go”).
Pronunciation[edit]
(Portugal) IPA(key): /dɨˈɣɾaw/
(Brazil) IPA(key): /deˈɡɾaw/
Hyphenation: de‧grau
Rhymes: -aw
Noun[edit]
degrau f (plural degraus)
step (of a staircase) or rung (of a ladder)
Apoie o pé nesse degrau.Rest your foot on this step.
(figurative) step (a distinct part of a progression)
Alternative forms[edit]
degrao (obsolete)
