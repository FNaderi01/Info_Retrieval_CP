Russian[edit]
Etymology[edit]
Inherited from Old East Slavic игра (igra), from Proto-Slavic *jьgra.
Pronunciation[edit]
IPA(key): [ɪˈɡra]
.mw-parser-output .k-player .k-attribution{visibility:hidden}Audio(file)
Noun[edit]
игра́ • (igrá) f inan (genitive игры́, nominative plural и́гры, genitive plural игр, related adjective иго́рный or игрово́й)
game
игра́ в мяч ― igrá v mjač ― ball game
a sport which is played (also спортивная игра)
(action): play in the following senses:
Activity for amusement only, especially among the young.
Similar activity, in young animals, as they explore their environment and learn new skills.
(ethology) "Repeated, incompletely functional behavior differing from more serious versions ..., and initiated voluntarily when ... in a low-stress setting."
An individual's performance in a sport or game.
acting, performance
игра́ актёра ― igrá aktjóra ― an actor’s performance
role-playing (also ролевая игра)
playing (a musical instrument)
игра́ на гита́ре ― igrá na gitáre ― playing the guitar
