Hungarian[edit]
Etymology[edit]
Back-formation from körül.[1] The kör- stem is from Proto-Finno-Ugric *kere (“circle, ring, hoop, tire”). Cognates include Komi-Zyrian kor (kor, “circle”) and Udmurt kuri (kuri, “circle”).[2]
Pronunciation[edit]
IPA(key): [ ˈkør]
Audio(file)
Rhymes: -ør
Noun[edit]
kör (plural körök)
(geometry) circle
Synonyms: karika (informal), gyűrű (literally, “ring”)
(society) circle, ring, company
Synonyms: társaság, szervezet
(sports, motor racing) round, lap
Synonyms: futam, forduló, menet, rund
(figurative) sphere, range, scope, field, area

Icelandic[edit]
Etymology[edit]
From Old Norse kǫr, from Proto-Germanic *karō (“concern, care”). See also Old Saxon cearu, Old English caru.[1]
Pronunciation[edit]
IPA(key): /kʰœːr/
Rhymes: -œːr
Noun[edit]
kör f (genitive singular karar, no plural)
a bed in which one lies bedridden

Turkish[edit]
Etymology[edit]
From Ottoman Turkish كور‎ (kör), borrowed from Persian کور‎ (kur).
Pronunciation[edit]
IPA(key): /køɾ/
Adjective[edit]
kör (comparative daha kör, superlative en kör)
blind
Synonym: âmâ
blunt, not sharp
of a place, having little or no visibility
(figurative) careless, unaware of what's happening
Derived terms[edit]
körce
körlük
Noun[edit]
kör (definite accusative körü, plural körler)
someone who is blind; unable to see
Synonym: âmâ
