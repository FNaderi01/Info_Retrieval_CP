		Trilling Islands (69°30′S 39°38′E﻿ / ﻿69.500°S 39.633°E﻿ / -69.500; 39.633Coordinates: 69°30′S 39°38′E﻿ / ﻿69.500°S 39.633°E﻿ / -69.500; 39.633) is a three islands at the south side of Skarvsnes Foreland, lying in Trilling Bay in the east part of Lutzow-Holm Bay. Mapped by Norwegian cartographers from air photos taken by the Lars Christensen Expedition, 1936–37, and named Trillingoyane (the triplet islands).
