Galician[edit]
Etymology[edit]
From Old Galician and Old Portuguese -eza, from Latin -itia(m).
Pronunciation[edit]
IPA(key): [ˈeθɐ], (western) [ˈesɐ]
Suffix[edit]
-eza f
-ness; -ity (appended to adjectives to form nouns meaning “the state of ...”, “the quality of ...”, or “the measure of ...”)
‎triste (“sad”) + ‎-eza → ‎tristeza (“sadness”)
Synonym: -ura

Portuguese[edit]
Pronunciation[edit]
(Portugal, Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈe.zɐ/
Homophone: -esa
Etymology 1[edit]
From Old Portuguese -eza, from Latin -itia(m).
Suffix[edit]
-eza f
-ness; -ity (appended to adjectives to form nouns meaning “the state of ...”, “the quality of ...”, or “the measure of ...”)
‎belo (“beautiful”) + ‎-eza → ‎beleza (“beauty”)
Synonyms[edit]
-ez, -idade, -idão
Derived terms[edit]
►  Portuguese words suffixed with -eza
Related terms[edit]
-ícia, -ice, -ície
Etymology 2[edit]
Suffix[edit]
-eza f
Obsolete spelling of -esa
Spanish[edit]
Etymology[edit]
From Latin -itia(m).
Suffix[edit]
-eza f (plural -ezas)
Attached to adjectives to form abstract nouns of that quality
‎grande (“grand, great”) + ‎-eza → ‎grandeza (“grandeur”)
